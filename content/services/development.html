// 02 / Perspective
What we hold to in development work.

Most engineering work fails its second year, not its first. The build ships, the team that built it disperses, and the next person inherits a stack that was perfect for shipping in 2024 and unmaintainable by 2026. We pick frameworks for the project's actual lifespan, not the agency's preferred toolkit.

If your internal team will own the code after we're gone, that constraint shapes every architectural decision we make. We'll say so out loud.

Performance, accessibility, and CMS-friendliness are not separate workstreams that get sequenced. They are three views of the same engineering decision. We build them in from week one — not because of a methodology pitch, but because retrofitting them later costs three times as much.

The toolkit is whatever fits. Not whatever's loudest on the conference circuit.

// 03 / What we ship
What shipping development with Modelic looks like.

Theme + template engineering

Stencil, Shopify Liquid, EDS, custom CMS templates. The presentation layer your editorial team interacts with daily — built for speed and editor friendliness.

Headless integrations

Next/Astro/SvelteKit frontends on commerce or content APIs. We use headless when the experience demands it, not because the AGENCY blog said to.

Performance work

Core Web Vitals, bundle audits, image optimization, edge caching. The numbers Google reports back to you, the numbers your conversion analytics report — same ones.

Accessibility implementation

WCAG 2.1 AA conformance baked in at the build phase, not bolted on at the audit phase. Engineers who use NVDA and VoiceOver daily.

CMS integration

Squarespace, WordPress, Contentful, Sanity, Adobe Experience Manager. We adopt the CMS your team already uses — or help you migrate if it's failing them.

Ongoing engineering support

Bug fixes, performance regressions, dependency updates, post-launch features. We stay through the warranty window — and into the second year if the work calls for it.
