// 02 / Perspective
What we hold to in accessibility work.

Most accessibility work fails because it gets retrofitted at the end. By the time someone runs an axe-core scan over the build, the architectural decisions that should have been accessibility decisions are already three sprints behind. We start at design — not at audit.

WCAG 2.1 AA conformance is the floor. The ceiling is whether someone using NVDA or VoiceOver can actually complete the task they came for. Those are different questions, and we answer the second one.

The audit is the warm-up. The work is the remediation, and the work after that is the training that lets your internal team maintain conformance after we're gone. The Utah Food Bank project — three years post-launch — has held WCAG 2.1 AA through two redesigns and a CMS migration. That's the goal: durability, not just a pass.

Accessibility done well is invisible. That's the point.

// 03 / What we ship
What shipping accessibility work with Modelic looks like.

Audits

Manual and automated, paired. Real users on real assistive tech — not just an axe-core scan. The audit names what to fix and what to deprecate.

Remediation

Code-level fixes, plus the architectural changes that prevent re-introduction. We don't just patch the symptom — we name the pattern.

Ongoing compliance

Annual re-audit, regression testing on every release, sustainability reviews when the site grows. Conformance isn't a milestone — it's a posture.

Design-stage review

Color contrast, focus order, motion sensitivity, language clarity — caught at design phase before they become refactor work. The cheapest accessibility audit is the one in Figma.

Training

Half-day workshops for design and engineering teams. We leave your team able to maintain conformance — and able to know when something is borderline.

Procurement support

VPATs, ACR documentation, RFP responses for accessibility-required contracts. The paperwork that demonstrates the work, when the work is done.

// 04 / Modern toolkit
Where AI earns its place in accessibility work.
Audit tooling that flags real issues, not noise. Alt-text generation paired with human review for context. Captioning for video at scale. AI is the warm-up — automation handles the obvious, and human auditors handle the judgment calls that automation will get wrong. We don't replace the audit with AI. We use AI to make the audit more thorough.
// 05 / Partnership
Inside your team, not across from it.

Accessibility engagements often pair us with an internal QA team and the lawyers who care about Section 508 or ADA compliance. We work alongside both — translating legal requirements into engineering decisions, and engineering decisions into legal documentation. Neither side has to learn the other's language to ship the project.
