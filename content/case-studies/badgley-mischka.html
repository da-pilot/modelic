// 03 / The brief
What Badgley Mischka came to us with.
"We needed a commerce site that didn't fight the editorial photography we've spent twenty years building. Most ecommerce themes treat a $4,000 gown the same as a $40 t-shirt. We needed a site that respected the work."
// 04 / Discovery
What we learned in the audit.
The existing storefront was a stock theme with photography compressed beyond recognition. The site loaded in two seconds and lost the brand entirely in the same two seconds. The customers Badgley Mischka spent the most acquiring — high-AOV, low-frequency, photo-led — were getting the worst experience.
We also found the merchandising team was working around the platform, not with it. Editorial collections were being assembled in a Google Doc and copy-pasted into product groupings every season. The platform had the tools — they were buried under three layers of admin chrome that no one had time to learn.
// 05 / Brand exploration
Photography first, everything else after.
We rebuilt the product detail page around a single decision: the photograph dominates. Type sits beside it, not on top of it. The "add to bag" call-to-action is where it needs to be — quietly present, never competing with the work the photo is doing.
The collection landing pages are editorial, not catalog. Each season has a story; the products are illustrations of the story. Conversion follows from the storytelling — not from the funnel optimization.
// 06 / What we decided
Three load-bearing calls.
We treated the merchandising team as the primary user. The customer-facing site is the visible work; the admin experience the merchandising team uses every day is the invisible work. We rebuilt both.
We dropped a feature. The original RFP included an AR try-on experience for accessories. After two weeks of prototyping we recommended cutting it — the customer base wasn't asking for it, and the engineering cost would have eaten the editorial budget. The Badgley Mischka team agreed.
We commissioned new photography for the responsive crops. The existing editorial photography was shot for print and didn't survive the responsive grid. We worked with the in-house team to commission a parallel shoot for digital. The cost was significant; the result is a site that doesn't apologize for being on the web.
