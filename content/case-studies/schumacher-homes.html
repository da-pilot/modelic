// 03 / The brief
What Schumacher Homes came to us with.
"A custom home is the largest purchase most of our customers will ever make. The decision happens over months. We needed a digital experience that respected the size of the decision — without slowing it down for the customer who already knew."
// 04 / Discovery
What we learned.
Schumacher's catalog of floor plans is the largest in the country. The previous site treated each plan as a static page — 480 pages, none of them configurable. Customers were doing the configuration in conversations with sales reps, by hand, in spreadsheets. The work was happening; it was happening offline, slowly, and invisibly.
We also found a customer pattern: people would research for weeks, decide on a floor plan, then change their mind once they realized the customizations they wanted weren't actually possible in the plan they'd picked. The site was making promises it couldn't keep.
// 05 / Build
A real configurator, not a glorified form.
We built a configurator that actually configures — every selection updates a 3D rendering, every constraint is enforced, every price is real. The customer sees what they're buying as they're buying it. No more conversations that end in disappointment.
The platform feeds back into Schumacher's sales pipeline. When a customer saves a configuration, the regional sales team sees a real specification — not a list of selections to interpret. The lead-to-close cycle compressed measurably.
// 06 / What we decided
Three load-bearing calls.
We rebuilt the catalog model first. Before the configurator, before the 3D pipeline, we spent twelve weeks restructuring how a floor plan is described in data. Customizations, constraints, regional variations — all relational, all expressible in the configurator. Without this, the rest of the project was impossible.
We integrated with the existing sales process, not around it. The sales team was the audience, not the obstacle. The new tool replaces the spreadsheets; it doesn't replace the relationship.
We launched a region at a time. Schumacher operates across multiple regions with different building codes. We rolled the configurator out region by region, validating with each regional sales team before expanding.
