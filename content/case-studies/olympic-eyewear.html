// 03 / The brief
What Olympic Eyewear came to us with.
"Wholesale is a different business than retail. Most ecommerce platforms treat it as a checkbox on the side of a retail product. We needed a platform that treated wholesale as the main thing — because for us it is."
// 04 / Discovery
What we learned.
Olympic's wholesale accounts were placing orders by phone, by fax, and by email — never through the website. The website existed; the wholesale buyers had learned to work around it. Every order placed offline cost the company in admin time and in lost analytical visibility.
We also found the buyer behavior was nothing like a retail customer's. Wholesale buyers come back weekly, place orders of 50–500 units, and expect to see their account-specific pricing without thinking about it. The site treated them like a first-time visitor every time.
// 05 / Build
Wholesale as the primary product.
We rebuilt the storefront as a wholesale-first experience. Account-specific catalogs, contract pricing, draft-order workflows, freight quoting at the line-item level. The retail experience still exists — for distributors who serve walk-in customers — but it's downstream of the wholesale flow, not upstream.
Buyers can place a 200-unit order in under three minutes. Reorders pull from order history. Account-level credit limits, payment terms, and shipping addresses propagate without rekeying.
// 06 / What we decided
Two load-bearing calls.
We rebuilt the customer model. Olympic's buyer base wasn't individuals — it was accounts with multiple authorized users, separate billing and shipping addresses, and account-level pricing. The platform's standard customer model couldn't represent that. We extended it.
We integrated with the ERP, not around it. Inventory, pricing, and credit limits are authoritative in the ERP. The storefront reads those values in real time. No nightly sync, no stale data, no overselling.
