// 03 / The brief
What Hugger Mugger came to us with.
"We ship yoga blocks. Not many people in the analytics space have built dimensional shipping logic for cork, foam, and bolsters at the same time. We needed a team that wouldn't treat shipping as an afterthought."
// 04 / Discovery
What we learned.
Hugger Mugger sells yoga products that are dense, heavy, and dimensionally awkward. A single bolster ships in a different freight class than a stack of blocks. The previous storefront calculated shipping by guessing — and the company was eating the difference on every shipment that crossed a zone boundary.
We also found the wholesale customer base — yoga studios buying for their classroom — was being sent through the same flow as a retail customer buying a single mat. The friction was costing real revenue and the studios were calling it in instead.
// 05 / Build
Real shipping math, real wholesale flows.
We integrated ShipperHQ for dimensional shipping calculations — the bolster math actually works now. Every product has real dimensions in the catalog, every shipment is quoted accurately, and the margin loss on freight has gone from a known problem to a non-problem.
The wholesale flow is its own product. Studio accounts get tiered pricing, draft-order workflows, and a customer experience that matches the buying volume. Retail customers see the storefront they expect; studios see the tools they need.
// 06 / What we decided
Two load-bearing calls.
We rebuilt the catalog data, not just the storefront. The product attributes that mattered — weight, dimensions, freight class — were either missing or wrong on three quarters of the catalog. We did the migration. It wasn't glamorous; it's the reason the rest of the build works.
We separated wholesale into a parallel customer experience. Same platform, same admin, very different flow. The studios noticed the next time they ordered.
