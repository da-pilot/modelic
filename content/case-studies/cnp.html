// 03 / The brief
What CNP came to us with.
"We're a cooperative. The brand had to feel like the people, not like a corporation. We needed a partner who would understand that distinction without us having to defend it every meeting."
// 04 / Discovery
What we learned.
CNP's membership had grown faster than the brand could keep up with. The visual identity was inherited from a much smaller cooperative twenty years earlier — beautiful, but unrecognizable in the new context. The members loved it for the wrong reasons.
The digital product layer — member accounts, ordering, distribution scheduling — had been built piece by piece over a decade. Each piece was reasonable; the whole was a maze. New members took weeks to learn the system. Long-time members were patient about it. We didn't want to bet the next decade on patient long-time members.
// 05 / Brand
A refresh, not a rebuild.
The bones of the original identity were the right bones — they just needed a contemporary treatment. We kept the wordmark, redrew its proportions, and built a type and color system around it that scales from member newsletter to delivery truck.
The digital product was a different conversation. We rebuilt the member experience as a single React app on top of the existing data — no migration risk, real reduction in friction. New-member onboarding now takes minutes, not weeks.
// 06 / What we decided
Two load-bearing calls.
We didn't replace the wordmark. The temptation in a brand refresh is to redraw everything. We argued the wordmark was a heirloom and treated it that way. The cooperative agreed.
We built the new app on top of the old data. Migration would have meant a six-month freeze on all new features. The cooperative couldn't afford that. We didn't need to.
