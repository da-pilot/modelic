// Modelic / How we work

Not a vending machine.

A craft design and engineering shop. Partners in sustainable growth — not perpetual retainers, not handed-off proposals, not industry-leading anything.

// 02 / What this isn't

We are not a vending machine. We want to be your partners in sustainable growth.

The agency model most clients have lived through is a transaction. You write a brief, you get a deck, you sign a contract, and a team you'll never see again ships some work. Then there's a retainer, and the retainer is the business model. We don't run that way — and we don't apologize for it.

Modelic is a small, senior team. Every project is led, designed, and engineered by the people you'd meet on the call. The contract has an end date. The relationship usually doesn't.

We grow by getting better at the work, not by getting bigger. We turn down projects that don't fit. We bring our craft to complement your team's expertise — and we say so out loud when there's something we're not the right shop for.

This page is the long version of that. The short version is: hire us because we excel at something you cannot do.

// 03 / Three things we hold to

The three tenets.

/ 01

We partner.

We're inside your team, not across the table from you. The cadence we work in is yours — your tools, your standup time zone, your decision rituals. We bring our process only where there's a gap.

Communication is the cornerstone. Projects live and die by it. If a project is going wrong, we say so on the next call — not in a status report two weeks later. If we're going to miss a date, we say it the moment we know.

We don't want to be your vendor. We want to be a small extension of your team. That changes how decisions get made, how scope evolves, how scope gets cut. The work that ships is better when there's no client/agency wall to perform across.

/ 02

We measure.

We build for outcomes you can name. Conversion on a specific page. Performance on a specific budget. Cost on a specific team. The metric that anchored the kickoff is the metric that anchors every decision through ship — and the metric we report against past launch.

If we can't measure it, we don't claim it. "Industry-leading" and "best-in-class" don't appear in our case studies for the same reason they shouldn't appear in yours.

This isn't optimization for its own sake. Real measurement keeps us honest about the work that didn't move the number we said we'd move. Sometimes we ship something that didn't pan out. We say so. The next thing works better because of it.

/ 03

We use modern tools.

Modern CMS, modern commerce, AI where it earns its place. The toolkit a small craft team should have in 2026 — not the one a 200-person agency is busy defending because they hired around it five years ago.

AI shows up as internal velocity, not as a feature we sell to your end users. Catalog migrations cut by 70%. Code review on every PR. Test scaffolding from real production traffic. Audit tooling that flags the obvious so the human auditors can spend their time on the judgment calls. We use it where it shortens an honest task.

We don't ship a chatbot inside your storefront because the conference deck said to. We don't put "AI-powered" anywhere on a marketing page. The bar for an AI feature in a shipped product is whether it would still be useful at ten times the cost.

// 04 / The cadence

What an engagement looks like from the inside.

/ 01

Kickoff

One full-day session. We agree on the metric we're moving — together — and write it on the whiteboard. Everything that follows is anchored to that one decision.

Week 1
/ 02

Discovery

User research, audit reads, internal-team conversations. We bring the question; the data tells us the answer. Discovery ends when we've named what to build, and what to leave on the table.

Weeks 2–3
/ 03

Build

Weekly demos, decisions documented in your tools, shippable increments every two weeks. We don't disappear into a Figma file for a month — the work is visible, in the browser, all the way through.

Weeks 4–10
/ 04

Handoff + warranty

Documentation, training, a Slack line for two months post-launch. Your internal team is set up to extend the work without inheriting our judgment calls as folklore.

Weeks 11–12+
