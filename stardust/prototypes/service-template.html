// 02 / Perspective

What we hold to in commerce work.

Most commerce work fails before it ships because the product team and the engineering team are sold on different success metrics. We close that loop in week one. Everyone in the room agrees on what we're moving — conversion on a specific page, AOV in a specific segment, time-to-checkout — and that number anchors every decision until launch.

That isn't a methodology pitch. It's the difference between a project that ships and a project that drifts.

Stencil, Shopify Liquid, headless composable architectures — the platform isn't the headline. The platform is whatever fits the buyer experience and the team that has to maintain it after we're gone. We've built on every flavor, and we'll tell you when one isn't right for what you're trying to do.

Commerce is the practice of selling well. Tooling serves that, not the other way around.

// 04 / Modern toolkit

Where AI earns its place in commerce work.

Catalog migrations are the obvious place — LLM-driven attribute extraction has cut manual mapping by ~70% on the projects we've used it on. Beyond that: search relevance tuning, copy variants for product detail pages, alt-text generation for accessibility audits. We use AI where it shortens an honest task. We don't ship a chatbot to your storefront because the brief said to.

// 05 / Partnership

Inside your team, not across from it.

Commerce work usually pairs us with an internal merchandising lead, a CRO analyst, and an engineering counterpart on the platform team. We adapt to your cadence. Weekly demos, shared boards, decisions documented in your tools — not ours. Communication is the cornerstone of every project we ship.
