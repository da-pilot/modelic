03 · Typography

04 · Voice

Plain-spoken, partner-minded, slightly self-effacing.

A craft agency that talks like a human collaborator, not a sales deck. Self-effacement ("we are not a vending machine") is a feature, not a bug.

Voice is

Plain-spoken Earnest Partner-minded Craft-respecting Slightly self-effacing

Voice is not

Salesy Hype-y Agency-jargon-laden Boastful Disposable / vending-machine
✓ Do
"Modelic is a full-spectrum design and development agency that cultivates smart strategies for branding, UX, e-commerce, and web accessibility."
Names what we do without superlatives. "Cultivates" lands warmer than "delivers" or "drives."
✗ Don't
"We're a world-class digital agency delivering best-in-class solutions."
Generic superlatives — exactly the agency-speak the brand defines itself against.
✓ Do
"We are not a vending machine. We want to be your partners in sustainable growth."
Self-effacing line that defines the relationship by naming what we won't be.
✗ Don't
"Unlock explosive growth with our proprietary methodology."
Hype + jargon. The brand's self-effacing register makes "unlock" and "explosive" feel like another voice entirely.
✓ Do
"Hire us because we excel in something you cannot do. We'll utilize our skills to complement your areas of expertise."
Acknowledges client's strengths first, ours second.
✗ Don't
"Submit your project request and our team will respond within 24 hours."
Vending-machine framing — exactly the relationship the brand says it isn't.
  • Lead with the partnership, not the deliverable.
  • Plain verbs over agency verbs: "help", "build", "design" beats "leverage", "deliver", "drive".
  • If a sentence could appear on a competitor agency's site verbatim, rewrite it.
  • Self-effacing lines are part of the voice — keep them.
  • Title-case headlines; sentence-case body. The wordmark stays lowercase.
05 · Tone

Calm and partner-minded across contexts.

Process pages are declarative. Case studies are story-led. Contact copy is warm and specific (real phone number, real hours).

01

To partner

Position Modelic as a collaborator inside the client's team, not a supplier across the table.

02

To respect craft

Honor the client's expertise; bring our craft to complement, not replace.

03

To be findable in plain language

Real phone number, real hours, real services. No cute placeholders.

Clever — sparingly

The "we are not a vending machine" line is the brand's allowance for a touch of wit. One per page, max.

Clear — default

Service nav, process tenets, contact lines, and CTAs are all plainly labelled.

06 · Motifs

The wave is the brand. Everything else supports it.

Modelic wave

Two interleaved sine curves — Console silhouette with a Wavelength accent riding behind it. Reads as oscillation, signal, model + logic.

Usage: Always paired with the wordmark in the primary lockup. May stand alone at small sizes (favicon, app icon) and may animate on first load. The refresh treats this mark as kinetic by default — see .impeccable.md.

Pale teal photo wash

Hero and case-study photography sits beneath a translucent Foam (#CCE4E4) overlay with a faint waveform line pattern on top. Effect is dreamy, considered, agency-calm.

Usage: Hero sections and case-study openers. Avoid on portrait close-ups where skin tones should read true. The refresh will reduce density of this wash — selected hero moments only.

123

Display numerals

Tenets and process steps marked with very large Inter 300 numerals set inside Foam panels and offset against Console copy.

Usage: Any list-of-N section: principles, process steps, services. One numeral per panel, set generously.

modelic

Lowercase wordmark

The wordmark is always set lowercase, no exceptions. Sentence-case "Modelic" is correct in body copy; the wordmark itself is fixed lowercase.

Usage: Logo lockups only. Never replace the wordmark with a typed "modelic" inside running copy — use the asset.

07 · Components

Sharp corners, uppercase action.

Three button patterns, all set in Inter 600 / 12.48px / uppercase. Border-radius is 0px across the system — a deliberate counterpoint to the curved wave mark.

Console-filled · Eyebrow-link · Nav. The refresh may revisit the 0px radius — it's a strong choice but reads as 2018-Squarespace today.

08 · Photography

Workspace and team-in-context.

People mid-action — typing, sketching — shot from a slightly elevated angle in cool, neutral light. Editorial calm, not stock photography.

✓ Do

  • Always overlay hero imagery with the Foam wash and a subtle waveform graphic.
  • Subjects mid-action (typing, sketching) rather than posed.
  • Cool to neutral light. Avoid warm golden-hour tones — they fight the teal.

✗ Don't

  • No isolated product shots on white.
  • No stock-feeling handshakes, conference rooms, or generic "team in a meeting" setups.
  • No saturated brand colors imported from clients into Modelic-owned imagery.

10 · Logo

Wave + lowercase wordmark.

Clear space

  • Maintain clear space equal to the cap height of the wordmark on all sides.
  • Minimum on-screen lockup width: 96px.

Don't

  • Don't place the wordmark on backgrounds that obscure the wave's teal accent.
  • Don't stretch, recolor the wordmark to non-Console values, or separate the wave from the wordmark in horizontal lockup.
  • Don't set the lockup smaller than 96px wide — the inter-wave detail collapses.
