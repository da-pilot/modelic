# Intent

The Contact page is the close. A visitor arriving here is ready (or close to ready) to start a project conversation; the page's job is to make that conversation as easy and as specific as possible. It also serves a second function: it is the **single source of truth** for Modelic's contact details — phone, email, hours, address — that the rest of the site reuses through the `closing-cta` fragment. Authoring the strings here authoritatively prevents drift across the site.

The page is **the warmest content on the site after `/team`** and the **least cluttered** of any page. It is short.

# Audience

A decision-maker who has decided (or is one click from deciding) to reach out. They've usually come from `/`, `/work`, or a case-study page. They are not browsing — they want to write a message or pick up a phone.

A small secondary audience: returning prospects checking that Modelic still has a working phone number, or candidates double-checking the studio location. The page accommodates them with the same content.

# Key Messages

1. **You can reach a real person quickly.** Specific phone, specific hours, specific inbox. No "submit a request and our team will get back to you within 24 hours" framing.
2. **The first message can be short.** The form asks only what's actually useful — discouraging long, pre-formatted RFP-style submissions in favor of a real conversation starter.
3. **Modelic responds personally, not automatically.** No auto-responder. The page sets the expectation.
4. **The studio is in Salt Lake City.** Specific location is a credibility signal — the agency is somewhere, not everywhere. Mountain Time hours follow.

# Calls to Action

- **Primary:** submit the contact form. The form *is* the page.
- **Secondary:** call **801-363-0101** during weekday hours. Phone is given the same visual weight as the form — not relegated to a footer.
- **Tertiary:** email a real inbox. `[TBD — Modelic to confirm: hello@modelic.io / projects@modelic.io / etc.]`. The address is shown in plain text (clickable mailto) — not behind a "request info" button.

# Tone

Warm and specific. The shortest, plainest copy on the site. Every line earns its place; nothing is pre-formatted agency-speak. The phrase *"We are here for you weekdays from 8:30 am - 5:30 pm Mountain Time"* from the current site is the directional anchor — keep that register, drop nothing about it.

Tonal rules layered on the site-wide tone:

- **No form-page boilerplate.** Forbidden: *"We'd love to hear from you"*, *"Drop us a line"*, *"Let's start a conversation about your needs"*, *"How can we help?"* (as a headline). The brand's existing *"Tell us about it!"* is closer — direct, friendly, not templated.
- **Field labels are warm.** *"What kind of work are you thinking about?"* beats *"Project Type."* *"Tell us about it"* beats *"Message."*
- **Hours and phone are specific.** Time zone always given. Phone formatted human-style: **801-363-0101**.
- **No "thank you for submitting" page** — the success state is a sentence on the same page (or a small modal) that names what happens next, specifically. *"We've got it. One of us will reply by the end of the next business day — by name, not from a noreply address."*
