# Intent

The Team page is where Modelic stops talking about the work and shows the people who do it. A visitor arriving here — primarily a decision-maker who landed via the homepage's Team Glimpse, secondarily a designer or engineer evaluating Modelic for a job — should leave with one durable impression: **the people you'd meet on the call are the people who'd ship the work**. The page exists to differentiate Modelic from agencies where the proposal team and the delivery team are different teams.

The page also doubles as the soft entry point for talent — a candidate who reads it and recognises themselves in the register should feel a low-friction next step toward `/careers`.

# Audience

**Primary:** decision-makers continuing from the homepage. They've seen the work; they want to know who shipped it. Specific question they're holding: *"Will the senior people stay on my project, or will I be handed off to a junior team?"*

**Secondary:** design and engineering talent considering Modelic for employment. Same content serves them — the team they'd join *is* the page. The `/careers` link is the secondary action.

The page is **not** for press, vendor sales, or general-interest browsing. The voice and depth are tuned for the two audiences above.

# Key Messages

1. **Modelic is small on purpose.** Headcount is a feature, not a limitation. The page makes this explicit.
2. **The senior people stay on the project.** No proposal-team / delivery-team split. The names and faces on this page are the names and faces a client works with through ship.
3. **Real craft, real range.** Every person on the page has a discipline (design, engineering, accessibility, commerce) and a craft note that signals they've thought about their work, not just done it.
4. **Partnership starts with people.** The site-wide "partner, not vendor" framing is most legible here — partnership is people, and these are them.

# Calls to Action

- **Primary:** "Start a project conversation" → `/contact`. Closing CTA fragment.
- **Secondary:** "Open roles" → `/careers`. Quiet eyebrow link in the footer area of the page; not the headline, not a banner. Only shown when there are open roles.
- **Tertiary:** "More work" → `/work`. Optional — a 2-tile case-study reuse block grounding the team in real projects shipped recently.

# Tone

The **warmest page** on the site. The partner register is dialed up; self-effacement is allowed in the team manifesto (more than the homepage, less than the Approach page). Real names, real photos, real craft notes — everything else feels generic next to that.

Tonal rules layered on the site-wide tone:

- **Bios are first-person where possible** — *"I lead the engineering work on commerce projects. Before Modelic I…"* — and short. 2–3 sentences each, not paragraphs. No "passionate about" anything.
- **Craft notes over career résumés.** What does each person *think* about their discipline? Not where they went to school.
- **Roles are specific.** *"Lead engineer, BigCommerce + headless"* beats *"Senior Developer."*
- **The page has one editorial moment per person, not a uniform grid of headshots.** Some portraits in studio, some at a desk, some mid-conversation. Variation is the point.
- Forbidden, layered on site-wide: "passionate", "rockstar", "ninja", "guru", "wizard", "team player", "we're a family". The current site's *"passionate, energetic and creative people"* line is the direction to evolve **away** from — keep the warmth, drop the vending-machine adjective stack.
